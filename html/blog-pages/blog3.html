Negative Impact of Technology in Watch Industry

January 19, 2025 | By Michael Samael


The introduction of technology has led watchmaking toward innovation yet it simultaneously generated operational difficulties for the industry. The current rise of smartwatches together with mass production has caused traditional watch craftsmanship to decrease which in turn affects watchmaking heritage and artistry. Mechanical watchmaking skills represent a major threat to the survival of this industry. Modern digital systems and automated technologies have reduced the market interest in hand-made mechanical watches. Traditional watchmaking suffers from declining appreciation because numerous expert craftsmen lose their crafts. Smartwatches have a short operational lifetime that poses a significant issue. Smartwatches decline in usefulness rapidly compared to mechanical watches because software updates and battery capabilities make them stop working after short periods of time. Electronics become waste products because of this which creates environmental pollution issues


Technological advancements now drive manufacturers to prioritize digital capabilities above craftsmanship. Today's watch industry gives precedence to configure notifications with fitness oversight instead of focusing on traditional watch craftsmanship. The transformation of the watch industry resulted in a diminishing of interest toward intricate mechanical watchmaking art since the introduction of modern technology. Smartwatches have emerged as a new security risk for users in the field of cybersecurity. The devices connect to smartphones while storing personal data which makes them susceptible to hacking attempts and privacy-threatening invasions. Traditional watches remain secure because they do not present any corresponding risks. Although production of traditional watches faces several obstacles there remains market value for collectors who maintain an interest in this craft. Technology's impact on the industry has created new problems in addition to vast industrial changes it has enabled because of its negative environmental impact and its security risks.
